# Biogeochemical Cycles

System-wide nutrient and chemical cycling processes in the miniBIOTA biosphere. Processes are labeled as **observed** (directly measured or witnessed) or **inferred** (logically expected but not yet directly measured).

---

## Water Cycle

### Observed
- Warm humid air rises into atmosphere tanks.
- Chilled rear glass condenses vapor.
- Condensate fills tipping reservoirs.
- Reservoirs tip when mass threshold reached.
- Rain falls into biome below.
- Rain occurs approximately every 2 to 3 weeks.

### Inferred
- Rain redistributes dissolved nutrients.
- Rain drives downward percolation in terrestrial biomes.
- Rain contributes to nutrient transport between elevation gradients.

---

## Nitrogen Cycle

### Observed
- No feeding. No nutrient supplementation.
- Legume present in Grassland.
- Deep sediment present in Seagrass Meadow.
- Detritus accumulation in Lake and Seagrass.

### Inferred Freshwater Processes
- Organic nitrogen mineralizes to ammonium.
- Aerobic nitrification occurs on root zones and substrate surfaces.
- Anaerobic denitrification occurs in deeper sediments.

### Inferred Marine Processes
- Denitrification likely active in deep seagrass substrate.
- Plankton predation alters nitrogen pathways.
- Larval capture reduces biomass export.

Nitrogen remains internally cycled under closure.

---

## Carbon Cycle

### Observed
- System is light-driven.
- Photosynthesis visible via plant growth and algae phases.
- High metabolic heat in biomes.

### Inferred
- CO2 fixed by plants and algae.
- CO2 released by respiration and decomposition.
- Oxygen fluctuates diurnally.
- Deep sediment likely oxygen-limited.

CO2 sensors are planned for direct validation.

---

## Calcium Cycle

### Observed
- Shell health improved in Lake after plant shift.
- Shell fragments present in Mangrove, Beach, and Seagrass substrates.
- Active shell-forming organisms present.

### Inferred
- Shell fragments provide carbonate buffering.
- pH stabilization influenced by plant community.
- Calcium availability influences shell condition.

---

## Salinity Control

### Observed
- Freshwater and marine systems isolated underground.
- Marine salinity appears stable.
- Beach and Seagrass exchange freely.

### Planned
- Engineered estuary to allow controlled density-separated interaction.
- Continuous freshwater rain push to regulate gradient.

---

## Nutrient Compression Risk

### Observed
- Seagrass detritus accumulation increasing.
- Limited export from marine basin.

### Inferred
- Closed systems may accumulate refractory organic matter.
- Long-term oxygen depletion risk in deep sediment.

Requires monitoring and possibly engineering adjustment.
