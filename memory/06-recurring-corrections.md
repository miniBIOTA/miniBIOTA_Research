# Recurring Corrections

Use this file to avoid known mistakes.

## Do Not Use Test Writes

Mistake: creating dummy Supabase records to inspect a function return value.

Correct behavior: read source, signatures, schema, or documentation instead.

Why it matters: test writes create real ecological records and can persist if not caught.

## Do Not Mix Story Threads And Open Loops

Mistake: treating every new ecological development as an open loop.

Correct behavior: story threads are internal tracking; open loops are audience-facing questions from published content.

Why it matters: mixing them corrupts both research tracking and public content continuity.

## Do Not Upgrade Uncertainty For Narrative Clarity

Mistake: turning `Possible` into `Likely`, `Likely` into `Confirmed`, or unresolved recruitment into establishment.

Correct behavior: keep the confidence label matched to the evidence.

Why it matters: public-facing claims and downstream records depend on conservative scientific boundaries.

## Do Not Skip The Research Question

Mistake: collecting sources, drafting public-safe wording, or writing handoff notes before defining the actual research question.

Correct behavior: state the question first, then gather sources, separate evidence categories, assign confidence, and record open questions.

Why it matters: unclear questions make it easier to overclaim, miss current-state checks, or turn a hypothesis into a conclusion.

## Do Not Treat Internal Research Labels As Public Permission

Mistake: treating `Likely`, `Possible`, `Speculative`, `Unknown`, `Needs microscopy`, or `Needs expert confirmation` as ready public language.

Correct behavior: use the label's public guidance. `Do not claim publicly yet` is a stop condition for public content, not a soft caution.

Why it matters: research can preserve uncertainty internally, but Content, Brand, Website, and Growth handoffs need public-safe wording that does not overclaim.

## Do Not Treat Migrated Docs As Live Data Without Checks

Mistake: using local overview counts as current species, observation, or thread totals.

Correct behavior: verify Supabase when current structured state matters.

Why it matters: miniBIOTA records change quickly.

## Do Not Reuse Legacy Status Language As Current Truth

Mistake: reusing status language from `docs/ecosystem_and_data_overview.md` or `docs/biome_profiles.md`, such as "thriving" or "established," as if it were current.

Correct behavior: treat those docs as reference/context and verify Supabase before reusing counts, species status, population outcomes, active loops/threads, chronicle state, or current biome condition.

Why it matters: dated ecology notes can make unresolved outcomes sound confirmed.
