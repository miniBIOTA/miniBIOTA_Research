# Biome Profiles

Detailed ecological profiles for each of the six biomes in the miniBIOTA system. Species listed here reflect historical composition — check Supabase `species` table for confirmed current populations.

---

## Lake Biome (Freshwater Aquatic System)

### Classification
- Fully aquatic freshwater biome.
- No filtration. No artificial circulation.
- Atmospherically routed to Lakeshore Atmosphere.
- Hydrologically connected to Lakeshore and Grassland.

### Substrate
- Quartz sand sourced from natural Florida lakes.
- Organic compounds introduced at collection.
- Surface detritus layer.
- Supports aerobic and anaerobic zones.

### Plants
- 1 Amazon Sword.
- Approximately 12 to 24 Tape Grass (Vallisneria americana).
- 1 Strap Leaf Sagittaria.
- Temporary macroalgae presence.
- Historical shift: Ludwigia removed, Tape Grass introduced. Current transitional algae phase present.

### Animals
- Fish: Flagfish removed 2026-04-05 to open microcrustacean niche.
- Crustaceans: Crayfish (established population; new hatchling wave observed 2026-04-16, indicating continued reproduction; actively consuming sagittaria roots 2026-04-17). Ghost Shrimp (*Palaemon paludosus*) — 4 confirmed individuals as of 2026-04-11. Larger Ostracods — second introduction 2026-04-16 (hundreds, full acclimation protocol); confirmed surviving overnight 2026-04-17 but population reduced; crayfish predation vs. acclimation shock unresolved. Freshwater Seed Shrimp (smaller ostracods) — present. Copepods (Calanoida id=65, Cyclopoida id=169) — active in water column, visible at 100x.
- Snails: Malaysian Trumpet Snail (id=79) — strong hatchling emergence 2026-04-17, reproducing. Leopard Ramshorn Snail (*Planorbella duryi*, id=165) — 6 individuals introduced 2026-04-08; freshly hatched individuals (at least 2) observed on front glass 2026-04-21, attributed to flagfish removal. Apple Snail (*Pomacea* sp., id=73) — 1 small individual persisting, unconfirmed. Freshwater Limpets, Bladder Snails.
- Microfauna: Daphnia sp. (likely ambigua, id=164) — thriving; lake water visibly clearing as of 2026-04-13. Mesostoma ehrenbergii (id=167) — eggs hatching in-system; active predatory threat to microcrustaceans, outcome unresolved. Freshwater Amphipod (id=66) — behavioral shift 2026-04-19: now openly swimming in water column; reduced predation pressure from flagfish removal allowing expanded presence. Substrate worms.
- New additions 2026-04-16: Water beetles — approximately a dozen individuals of multiple unidentified species; species ID pending.
- Ghost Shrimp (*Palaemon paludosus*, id=67): zoea observed 2026-04-19 — first documented larval stage in miniBIOTA; potential second generation underway (story thread 21).
- Bivalves: Asian Clam — extirpated, last seen 2025-11-02.

### Observations
- Biome sealed April 2026. Water clearing as of 2026-04-13 following Daphnia establishment and flagfish removal.
- No mechanical flow. Food web now actively developing — 7 open loops tracking outcome.
- Crayfish observed digging beneath substrate to consume sagittaria roots 2026-04-17; dying leaves entering detritivore cascade.
- Ostracod reintroduction (second attempt, 2026-04-16) using full acclimation protocol; first attempt 2026-04-08 lost to likely acclimation shock.

### Role
- Freshwater nutrient processor.
- Nitrogen cycling engine.
- Calcium buffering indicator.
- Source of humid air to atmosphere system.

## Lakeshore Biome (Transitional Freshwater Zone)

### Classification
- Aquatic-to-terrestrial gradient.
- Atmospherically connected to Lakeshore Atmosphere.
- Hydrologically connected to Lake and Grassland.

### Substrate
- Base quartz sand. Soil layer above. Organic plant matter at upper slope. Gradual moisture gradient.

### Plants
- Ludwigia (dominant). Dollarweed. Possible Torpedo Grass. Unidentified broadleaf ground cover.

### Animals
- Aquatic overlap: Crayfish, Freshwater shrimp, Snails, Microcrustaceans.
- Terrestrial roamers: Florida wood cockroaches, Caribbean hermit crabs, Mangrove tree crab, Isopods, Springtails, Crypt ants, Field cricket, Smoky oak millipede, Red house spiders.
- Amber Snail (*Succinea* sp., id=172) — approximately 75 individuals introduced 2026-04-17; semi-aquatic shoreline detritivore. ~50 of 75 observed on upper glass 2026-04-18 with mucus trails biome-wide; no predation from tree crab observed. Egg clusters confirmed on glass 2026-04-19 — reproduction underway. One individual confirmed in lowland meadow 2026-04-21. Story thread 19 tracking establishment.
- **Common Crypt Ant** (*Hypoponera opacior*, id=47) — colony established in miniBIOTA ~3.5 years. Winged individuals (alates) observed 2026-04-24, indicating colony entering reproductive phase. Nuptial flight anticipated. Story thread 28 tracking.
- **Ridgeback Sand Grasshopper** (id=157) — observed feeding on Ludwigia in lakeshore biome 2026-04-26; first documented instance of this species consuming Ludwigia within the system. Species confirmed ranging between lakeshore and lowland meadow biomes.

### Role
- Nutrient mixing zone. Nursery habitat via dense Ludwigia. Transition corridor. Buffer between aquatic and upland systems.

## Grassland Biome (Terrestrial Freshwater-Linked System)

### Classification
- Fully terrestrial. Receives direct rain. Hydrologically drains to Lakeshore. Atmospherically connected to Grassland Atmosphere.

### Substrate
- Thick organic-rich soil from Orlando lake region. Clay-like density. Moisture-retentive.

### Plants
- Unidentified vine legume (likely nitrogen-fixing). Mexican Primrose. Multiple grass species. Small flowering broadleaf plants. Seed-bank driven spontaneous succession.

### Fungal Presence
- Visible but limited mycelium. Desired expansion for improved decomposition.

### Animals
- Same terrestrial roaming network. High cross-biome movement.
- **Ridgeback Sand Grasshopper** (id=157) — actively feeding 2026-04-19; droppings forming piles in some areas. Isopods (Woodlouse id=46, Unknown Terrestrial Isopod id=173) observed actively consuming droppings — detrital food web link established.
- **Amber Snail** (*Succinea* sp., id=172) — first individual confirmed in lowland meadow 2026-04-21, feeding on glass. Long-term persistence in drier conditions under observation.

### Role
- Upland nutrient generator. Nitrogen fixation site. Rain infiltration and downhill water redistribution. Freshwater-side terrestrial stabilizer.

## Mangrove Forest Biome (Marine-Influenced Terrestrial System)

### Classification
- Salt-influenced terrestrial biome. Atmospherically connected to Mangrove Atmosphere. Hydrologically isolated underground from Grassland. Functions as freshwater-marine chemical boundary.

### Substrate
- Quartz sand mixed with crushed shells. Sourced from Banana River banks. Initially low organic content. Organic accumulation increasing via detritivore activity. Isopods consume mangrove leaves and deposit waste underground, gradually increasing soil organic density.

### Plants
- 1 Black Mangrove. 1 White Mangrove. Coin Vine. Brazilian Pepper (planned removal due to invasive status).

### Animals
- Mangrove Tree Crab. Terrestrial roaming organisms shared with other terrestrial biomes. Fiddler crabs occasionally enter but not dominant.
- **Florida Woods Cockroach** (*Eurycotis floridana*, id=26) — juvenile observed 2026-04-22 on mangrove branch, indicating a second generation has emerged within miniBIOTA. Second individual found deceased in red house spider web — active predation confirmed. Population status: Vulnerable. Story thread 25 tracking whether this generation establishes.

### Role
- Salt-tolerant terrestrial anchor. Soil-building via leaf litter processing. Chemical firewall between freshwater and marine systems. Precursor to future engineered estuary integration.

## Beach Biome (Marine Shoreline System)

### Classification
- Marine shoreline transition zone. Receives rain from Beach Atmosphere. Hydrologically connected to Seagrass Meadow. Erosion-active landscape.

### Substrate
- Quartz beach sand. Crushed shells. Sloped shoreline profile. High erosion susceptibility.

### Plants
- Beach grass species. Silverhead (coastal plant). 1 Red Mangrove. Additional species documented externally.

### Animals
- 4 Fiddler Crabs (1 male, 3 females). Mangrove tree crab (occasional). Hermit crabs. Terrestrial roamers. Marine invertebrates from Seagrass.
- **Gulf Marsh Crab** (*Sesarma schubarti*, id=174) — male introduced 2026-04-22 from brackish tidal ditch, Spring Hill FL. Confirmed active 2026-04-26 after 2-day absence. Both herbivory (grasping and consuming live grass blades) and opportunistic omnivory (feeding on detached fiddler crab leg) documented. Significant grazing role in managing grass abundance. Story thread 22 tracking establishment.
- **Ladder Hornsnail** (*Cerithidea scalariformis*, id=175) — introduced 2026-04-23. Semi-aquatic, shoreline-adjacent niche. Story thread 23 tracking.
- **Eastern Melampus** (*Melampus bidentatus*, id=176) — introduced 2026-04-23. Immediately observed feeding on dead grass detritus — strong establishment signal. Story thread 24 tracking.
- **Lawn Shrimp** (id=141) — reintroduced 2026-04-22. Prior attempt failed due to insufficient detritus; current organic material levels may now support establishment.

### Observed Instability
- Active shoreline erosion. Burrowing destabilization. Sediment displacement. Rain and marine interaction altering slope.

### Role
- Marine interface zone. Burrow-mediated sediment mixer. Structural tension zone. Mechanical instability variable.

## Seagrass Meadow Biome (Marine Aquatic System)

### Classification
- Fully aquatic marine biome. Deep substrate benthic system. Atmospherically routed to Beach Atmosphere. Most biodiverse biome.

### Substrate
- Deep quartz sand from submerged marine areas. Crushed shells dispersed. Thick benthic profile. Anaerobic zones likely at depth. Increasing detritus accumulation from seagrass die-off.

### Plants and Algae
- Shoal Grass (dominant). Macroalgae — expanded to dominant surface layer at far end of tank as of 2026-04-13; ongoing succession tension with shoalgrass (loops 8 and 9). Amphipod surge and one surviving variegated sea urchin are the primary self-correction candidates.

### Animals
- Crustaceans: Porcelain crabs. Mottled Shore Crab (id=18) — 2 individuals approaching 2-year tenure as of 2026-04-21; functioning primarily as herbivores; mud crab introduction being considered to explore a different feeding strategy. Small unidentified hermit crab, 1 orange claw hermit crab. Eelgrass isopods (reproducing). Marine Scud / Amphipods (id=36) — major population surge observed 2026-04-19, likely thousands in benthic zone; potential grazing pressure on macroalgae (loops 8 and 9 advanced). Marine glass shrimp (actively releasing larvae).
- Mollusks: 2 species Cerith snails, Slipper snails, Lightning Nerite snails (declining), 3 small mussels, Possible buried clams, Flipper snails (reproducing). **Scorched Mussel** (*Brachidontes exustus*, id=101) — 3 individuals collected 2026-04-22 from brackish tidal ditch; drip-acclimating as of 2026-04-22. Story thread 26 tracking establishment.
- Annelids: Lug worm, Ragworms, Hairworms.
- Cnidarians: Small anemones (abundant).
- Echinoderms: **Variegated Sea Urchin** (*Lytechinus variegatus*, id=178) — 2 small individuals collected and introduced 2026-04-26 to graze macroalgae and process shoal grass detritus. Both showed severe lethargy after introduction and initially appeared dead or near death by 2026-04-28; one individual was confirmed alive and active on 2026-04-29 and has been observed feeding on Graceful redweed. Story thread 27 tracking. Loop 9 remains open and advancing.

### Reproductive Dynamics
- Frequent larval release. Porcelain crabs likely capture planktonic larvae. Recruitment limited. High reproduction, low successful settlement.

### Observed Tension
- Accumulation of dead seagrass blades. Limited nutrient export. Potential long-term detritus compression risk.

### Role
- Marine nutrient processor. Larval production engine. Anaerobic sediment reactor. Organic accumulation basin.
