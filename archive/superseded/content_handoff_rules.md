# Content Handoff Rules

`miniBIOTA_Research` supports `miniBIOTA_Content` by supplying accurate, qualified, source-backed material. It does not own final public storytelling by default.

## Division Of Labor

Research owns:

- Species research.
- Identification support.
- Ecological mechanisms.
- Claim checks.
- Source and citation notes.
- Uncertainty labels.
- Experiment background.
- Closed-system risk analysis.
- Research briefs.

Content owns:

- Final YouTube scripts.
- Titles and thumbnails.
- Captions and social posts.
- Platform metadata.
- Content calendars.
- Episode planning and story structure.
- Polished website copy.
- Finished public species cards.

Research may draft public wording only when the user explicitly asks or when a brief includes a short "suggested content-safe wording" section.

## Default Handoff Format

Create a research brief under `research/research-briefs/` using `skills/content-handoff-brief/reference/research-brief-template.md`.

Each handoff should include:

- Plain-language summary.
- Strongest verified points.
- Uncertainty notes.
- Source-backed claims.
- Suggested content-safe wording.
- Phrases to avoid.
- Open research questions.

## Suggested Content-Safe Wording

Suggested wording should be:

- Accurate.
- Brief.
- Qualified where needed.
- Clear enough for a non-specialist.
- Weaker than or equal to the evidence, never stronger.

Examples:

- Use: "The larvae suggest the ghost shrimp reproduced, but juvenile survival is still unresolved."
- Avoid: "The ghost shrimp population is established" unless recruitment has been confirmed.

## Phrases To Avoid

Add phrases to avoid when they:

- Overstate evidence.
- Collapse an inference into a fact.
- Claim species-level identification without enough support.
- Promise ecological stability.
- Turn a temporary observation into a population outcome.
- Imply causation when only correlation is known.

## Source Expectations

Do not invent citations. If a source is missing, say so. If a source supports a general mechanism but not the miniBIOTA-specific case, make that distinction explicit.

## Handoff Checklist

Before sending research to Content:

- Current miniBIOTA state was verified if the claim depends on live records.
- Observed, inferred, and speculative points are separated.
- Uncertainty labels are present.
- At least the strongest claims have source notes.
- Public-safe wording is included.
- Unsafe or overstrong phrases are listed.
- Open questions are preserved.
