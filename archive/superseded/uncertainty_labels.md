# Uncertainty Labels

The Research Agent preserves uncertainty instead of forcing certainty. Use these labels in species research, identification notes, mechanism explanations, claim checks, and research briefs.

## Standard Labels

| Label | Use When | Public Content Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| `Confirmed` | Direct miniBIOTA evidence, reliable documentation, or strong source-backed identification supports the claim. | Can be used publicly with normal caution. |
| `Likely` | Evidence points strongly in one direction, but a final check, clearer image, measurement, or source is missing. | Public wording should include softeners such as "likely" or "appears to." |
| `Possible` | The claim is plausible and partially supported, but alternatives remain realistic. | Public wording should frame it as a possibility, not a conclusion. |
| `Speculative` | The idea is a hypothesis or interpretation with limited evidence. | Do not present as fact. Use only if the speculative nature is explicit. |
| `Unknown` | Evidence is missing, contradictory, or not yet reviewed. | Do not make a public claim beyond saying it is unknown. |
| `Needs microscopy` | Visual or behavioral evidence is not enough; microscopy is needed for confidence. | Do not present species-level identification publicly. |
| `Needs expert confirmation` | The ID or claim needs review by a specialist or authoritative source. | Use broader taxonomic or descriptive wording until confirmed. |
| `Do not claim publicly yet` | The claim is too uncertain, risky, unsupported, or easily misunderstood for public use. | Keep out of scripts, captions, website copy, and polished species cards. |

## Required Separations

Always separate:

- What was observed in miniBIOTA.
- What is known from general scientific literature.
- What is inferred from the observation.
- What is plausible but unconfirmed.
- What should not be said publicly yet.

## Public Wording Rules

- Do not turn `Likely` into `Confirmed`.
- Do not turn `Possible` into `Likely` for narrative clarity.
- Do not use a species name when only genus, family, or descriptive ID is supported.
- Do not imply that a source confirms a miniBIOTA-specific outcome unless it directly does.
- When a phrase could mislead the audience, add it to "Do not say" in the claim check or brief.

## Example

Observation: small transparent larvae were visible in the lake after ghost shrimp reproduction.

Possible labels:

- `Confirmed`: ghost shrimp reproduced if documented adults, brooding, and zoea were observed.
- `Unknown`: juvenile recruitment if larvae disappeared and no juveniles have been confirmed.
- `Do not claim publicly yet`: the ghost shrimp have established a self-sustaining population, unless later evidence confirms recruitment.
